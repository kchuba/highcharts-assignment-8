Social Media Use in the United States

By Kirsten Chuba

Within the last 10 years, social networks have rapidly transformed the world as we know it. Every year more and more American adults start using a form of social media; according to Pew Resource Center, 76 percent of Internet-using adults used social networks as of 2015.

As shown by the charts below, social media use for all age groups increased from 2010 to 2015, and not just in the younger age groups: 67 percent of Internet users 50-64 use social media, as do 56 percent of those 65 and older as of 2015. There are also a diverse number of social networks available for use with something for everyone; according to the data, those with a high school education or lower tend to use MySpace and Facebook, while those with Bachelor's or graduate school degrees more frquently use LinkedIn and Twitter.

As use of these social networks increases across the board and new social networks are created, the world becomes more interconnected and information is shared more easily. Social media has proven to be something people of all ages and education levels can use and enjoy, and has led to better and faster communication in the U.S. and around the world.
